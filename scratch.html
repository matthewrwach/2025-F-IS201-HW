Breast Cancer Awareness Game

My grandma battled breast cancer twice, so when I got the chance to help plan a Breast Cancer Awareness game for BYU Women’s Soccer, I knew I wanted to make it special.

My goal was simple: create a night where fans felt connected to the team and to the cause. I wanted people to share their stories, honor loved ones, and feel like they were part of something meaningful, not just watching a game.

At halftime, we shared a video of the team talking about who they play for. After that, fans posted photos holding signs with the names of the people they were honoring. Seeing players and fans come together like that turned the night into something way more memorable than a typical game.

This was the process

  1. Project Planning & Coordination
    • Collaborated with colleagues Harlee Woods and Hallie Knudtson to plan the Breast Cancer Awareness theme game, coordinating activities and assigning responsibilities for the event.
  2. Content Creation & Storytelling
    • Coordinated with the social media team to create a video featuring team members sharing their personal connections to breast cancer, humanizing the players and creating an emotional anchor for the theme.
  3. Interactive Experience Design
    • Designed the poster activity where fans could write about who they're fighting for, creating a meaningful way for fans to participate and share their own stories.
  4. Technology Integration
    • Configured Cue's Post Up software and designed graphics for the half-time moment, allowing fans to take photos with their posters and share them on the video board.
  5. Visual Atmosphere & Execution
    • Created pink ribbon loops to hang around the stadium and coordinated the overall game-day experience, including the half-time moment and fan participation activities.

It was a great reminder of how powerful sports can be, and I’m grateful I got to be part of it.

And shoutout to our soccer team as they head into the second round of the NCAA tournament. #winwheneveryoneiswatching
